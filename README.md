**Mental Health Corpus – Zipf Analysis**

**Overview**
In order to examine word frequency patterns and assess Zipf's law, this project examines a text dataset related to mental health.

**Dataset**
Source: Kaggle Mental Health Corpus

Contains user-generated mental health text

 **Methods**
Tokenized and cleaned text using NLTK

Counted word frequencies

Identified top 200 words

Calculated how many words make up 50% of the corpus

Created frequency and log-log plots

**Key Results**
299 words account for 50% of all text

Top words: im, like, want, know, feel

Distribution is highly skewed

**Conclusion**
According to Zipf's Law, the majority of words appear seldom in the corpus, whereas a tiny number of terms predominate. Additionally, the dataset includes first-person, emotive language that is common in conversations on mental health.
